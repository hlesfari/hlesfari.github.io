About

Since October 2019, I'm a PhD student in Computer Science at Université Côte D'Azur, under the guidance of Frédéric Giroire.

I'm a member of COATI, a joint project-team between INRIA Sophia Antipolis Méditerranée and the I3S laboratory of the CNRS.

My thesis lies at the intersection of graph representation learning and combinatorial network optimization. The aim is to understand how combining graph-based approaches with machine learning techniques can be leveraged to solve efficiently network optimization problems such as dynamic resource allocation and anomaly detection.

I have a broad range of interests in artificial intelligence that takes a broad view of the field and the many other areas it shapes and that in turn shapes it.
I am currently interested, but not limited to, in research at the intersection of AI and Computational Neuroscience.
